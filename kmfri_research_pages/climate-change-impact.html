Climate Change Impact

KMFRI’s Climate Change program conducts research to understand how global warming affects Kenya’s marine and freshwater ecosystems. This supports mitigation planning and adaptation strategies in vulnerable coastal and aquatic regions.

Key Research Areas

Sea Level Rise

Monitoring coastline changes and assessing flood risks in low-lying zones.

Ocean Acidification

Measuring pH variations and their effects on coral reefs and shellfish.

Temperature Trends

Tracking surface water temperature shifts and marine species migration.

Livelihood Vulnerability

Studying how climate shifts affect fisheries, aquaculture, and coastal communities.

Milestones

  • Climate risk mapping in Kenya’s coastal counties
  • Policy briefs submitted to the Ministry of Environment and Blue Economy
  • International collaboration with UNEP and IOC-UNESCO on ocean resilience
