Freshwater Research

Scientific studies supporting sustainable management of Kenya's lakes and rivers

About Our Freshwater Research

KMFRI conducts comprehensive research on Kenya's freshwater ecosystems, focusing on Lake Victoria, Lake Turkana, and major river systems to support fisheries management, water quality protection, and ecosystem conservation.

Fisheries Management

Stock assessments, fishing gear selectivity, and sustainable harvest strategies for freshwater fisheries.

Aquatic Ecology

Studies on food webs, invasive species impacts, and biodiversity conservation in freshwater systems.

Water Quality

Monitoring pollution, eutrophication, and developing remediation strategies for freshwater bodies.

Major Research Sites

Lake Victoria

Africa's largest lake supporting over 30 million people through fisheries and water resources.

Lake Turkana

World's largest permanent desert lake and UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Tana River Basin

Kenya's longest river supporting agriculture, hydropower, and biodiversity.

Lake Naivasha

Freshwater lake supporting floriculture industry and diverse wildlife.

Key Achievements

  • Development of sustainable fishing guidelines for Lake Victoria
  • Restoration programs for endangered freshwater species
  • Early warning systems for harmful algal blooms
  • Community-based management models for shared water resources
