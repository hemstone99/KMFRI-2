Socioeconomics Research

KMFRI's Socioeconomics division studies the economic, social, and cultural dynamics affecting fisheries and aquatic communities. The research informs sustainable development, resource management, and policy interventions at both community and national levels.

Focus Areas

Economic Valuation

Evaluating the economic contributions of fisheries and aquaculture to household income and GDP.

Livelihood Studies

Assessing fishing communities' wellbeing, food security, and dependence on aquatic resources.

Policy Support

Providing evidence-based recommendations for fisheries policy, licensing, and governance frameworks.

Gender & Youth

Understanding the roles and empowerment of women and youth in the blue economy.

Milestones

  • Socioeconomic baseline studies in 40+ coastal and lake regions
  • Development of national fisheries household surveys
  • Policy briefs adopted by Ministry of Blue Economy and Maritime Affairs
