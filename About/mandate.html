Our Mandate

The legal foundation and scope of our marine research mission

Legal Mandate

The Kenya Marine and Fisheries Research Institute (KMFRI) was established under the Science and Technology Act, Cap 250 of the Laws of Kenya (now repealed) through Legal Notice No. 154 of 1979. Our current mandate is derived from the Science, Technology and Innovation Act, 2013.

Core Functions

  • Conduct marine and freshwater fisheries research
  • Monitor and assess aquatic ecosystems
  • Develop sustainable resource management strategies
  • Provide scientific advice to policymakers
  • Disseminate research findings to stakeholders

Geographic Scope

  • Kenya's 200 nautical mile Exclusive Economic Zone
  • Coastal and marine environments
  • Inland lakes and river systems
  • Brackish water ecosystems
  • Transboundary water bodies

Research Focus Areas

  • Fisheries science and aquaculture
  • Marine biodiversity conservation
  • Coastal zone management
  • Climate change impacts
  • Marine biotechnology

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

"A prosperous nation with healthy aquatic ecosystems supporting sustainable blue economy."

Our Mission

"To generate knowledge and innovations for sustainable utilization and management of aquatic resources through research, capacity building and partnerships."
