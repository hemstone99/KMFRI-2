Our History

Over four decades of marine research excellence in Kenya

1979 - Establishment

KMFRI was established through Legal Notice No. 154 of 1979 as a state corporation under the Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock Development.

1980s - Foundational Research

Conducted baseline surveys of Kenya's marine ecosystems and established the first marine research stations along the coast.

1990 - Headquarters Relocation

Moved headquarters from Mombasa to its current location in Mtwapa to accommodate growing research programs.

2002 - Lake Victoria Expansion

Established the Kisumu Research Center to address freshwater fisheries research needs in Lake Victoria.

2013 - ISO Certification

Achieved ISO 9001:2008 certification for quality management systems in research services.

2020 - Blue Economy Focus

Realigned research priorities to support Kenya's Blue Economy initiatives and sustainable development goals.

Our Legacy

From humble beginnings with a small team of marine biologists, KMFRI has grown into a regional leader in marine and freshwater research. Our scientists have:

  • Discovered and documented over 500 marine species in Kenyan waters
  • Developed sustainable fishing practices adopted throughout East Africa
  • Pioneered coral reef restoration techniques now used internationally
  • Trained generations of marine scientists working across Africa
